How these numbers work

Where game-i's estimates come from — and why they change.

This site doesn't measure or estimate anything itself — it simply re-presents the figures game-i.daa.jp already publishes, in a more readable form (per-banner charts, rankings, and translated character names). All numbers and the methodology below are game-i's.

What the number is

An estimate of how much a banner earned from mobile gacha in Japan, shown here in yen. game-i reports it in “G” — a deliberately vague unit it says just means “about” (〜ぐらい). By common convention 1億G ≈ ¥100 million (so 1G ≈ ¥1), which is how the yen figures here are derived.

Where it comes from

game-i records each app's App Store / Google Play top-grossing rank every day at midnight (JST), converts that daily rank into an estimated daily revenue using a model calibrated against companies' publicly disclosed earnings, and adds up the days a banner runs. It's a rank → revenue estimate, not reported sales.

How a day's revenue is split between banners

The model assumes all of the app's estimated revenue that day is gacha revenue, and assigns it to whatever banners are running:

  • Banners running at the same time split the day equally.
  • A banner's first day is weighted up 80%, its last day down 80% (launch rush vs. wind-down).
  • Busy periods (New Year, Golden Week) run many banners at once, so each individual banner can look lower than it really earned.
  • Rank history only exists from 2018, so nothing older can be counted.

On the charts, a hatched section of a bar (and a “⇄ shared” tag) marks the portion of a run that overlapped another banner — i.e. revenue that was split. This shows up mostly on event games like FGO or Arknights; HoYo titles usually merge simultaneous characters into one “A&B” banner, so they rarely split.

Does it change after day 1?

Yes — but mostly in the first few days. Each day the banner is live adds that day's estimate from the game's current top-grossing rank. Because a banner spikes to its highest rank at launch and then steadily falls, most of the increase lands in the opening days; after that the daily additions get small and the total barely moves. It stops changing once the game drops out of the tracked ranking (below ~rank 200) or the banner ends.

What's covered — and what isn't

Japan only, iOS (App Store, incl. iPad) + Android (Google Play). It excludes overseas stores, PC and browser versions (DMM / mobage / GREE), and merchandise — so a game's true worldwide revenue is meaningfully higher than what you see here.

How accurate is it?

Directional, not exact. game-i itself calls the method どんぶり勘定 — a deliberate back-of-the-envelope built from statistics, regression analysis and machine prediction, with no guarantee of matching official figures. Known blind spots it discloses:

  • Days below ~rank 200 can't be tracked and are counted as ¥0 — weak banners get underestimated.
  • An app holding #1 can earn far beyond the model's ceiling (青天井), so monster banners get underestimated too.
  • The model is regressed from iOS ranks — if a game's Android rank diverges a lot, the estimate skews.
  • iOS-only apps should be read at about half the shown value; stores like Amazon/mobage aren't included at all.

It's better for top-ranked titles (more disclosed earnings to calibrate against) and rougher for smaller ones. Use these numbers to compare banners, not as official figures.

The “JP store rank today” tile

Each game's tiles include its current top-grossing rank (iOS & Android, Japan) straight from game-i, with a two-month sparkline of the daily iOS rank — the raw ingredient every estimate on this page is cooked from. Gaps in the line are days below the trackable ~top 200.

Reading a banner's daily rank curve

Click any banner to see its daily iOS top-grossing rank across the whole run (#1 = top of the chart). A few things to keep in mind:

  • Rank is snapshotted once, at midnight JST — so a banner's launch day can read below-200 if it went live after that snapshot; the spike shows up the next day.
  • Breaks in the line are days the app sat below the trackable ~top 200 — game-i can't see those and counts them as ¥0.
  • It's iOS only: game-i keeps a daily history for the App Store but only a live snapshot for Google Play, so there's no daily Android curve.
  • For a banner still running, the curve stops at today and the figures shown are the running totals so far, not the final ones.

The banner card also shows an estimated revenue build-up. game-i reports only one total per banner, so this splits that total across the run by each day's rank (using game-i's published rank→revenue curve) purely to illustrate how the total accumulated. The per-day yen is a reconstruction — the run's total still equals game-i's own figure — not a separately reported number.
